My calisthenics profession can be written in a short story. In the blink of an eye, my turn is up. I grip the bar harder than ever before, sweating from my head to my toe. My dangling feet, shaking from terror and the burst of excitement consuming me exponentially. Off I go into the air, propelling my body using my core. My mind is free and I forget about the competition. This is the definition of fun. It felt as if my strength was restored and that maybe, just maybe, God was holding me up by my waist, keeping me on the pull-up bars, an angel on my shoulder. I felt beautiful. It was one of the most beautiful moments of my life. In the end, I finally realized that winning or losing was not the greatest point of competition. Competition was just an environment to humble myself, challenge myself, and most importantly, to have fun. My calisthenics journey is now soaring and I am constantly getting better. I am willing to train people and have fun while doing so. Helping others along with helping myself, it is such an honor.
